## Products and Services

### Term Life Insurance
- Coverage: $50,000 – $2,000,000
- Terms: 10, 20, or 30 years
- Best for: Young families, mortgage protection, income replacement
- Average premium: $30–$150/month depending on age and health
- Top carriers: Protective, Banner, North American

### Whole Life Insurance
- Coverage: $25,000 – $1,000,000
- Lifetime coverage with guaranteed cash value growth
- Best for: Estate planning, supplemental retirement, legacy building
- Average premium: $150–$600/month
- Top carriers: Penn Mutual, MassMutual, New York Life

### Final Expense / Burial Insurance
- Coverage: $5,000 – $50,000
- Simplified issue (no medical exam)
- Best for: Seniors 50-85 wanting to cover funeral costs
- Average premium: $30–$100/month
- Top carriers: Mutual of Omaha, ANICO, Foresters

### Indexed Universal Life (IUL)
- Flexible premium, cash value tied to market index
- Coverage: $100,000 – $5,000,000
- Best for: High earners wanting tax-advantaged growth
- Top carriers: National Life Group, Pacific Life, Transamerica

### Group Life Benefits
- Employer-sponsored coverage for businesses with 5+ employees
- Typically 1-2x annual salary per employee
- Voluntary supplemental options available
- Top carriers: Principal, Guardian, Unum

### Annuities
- Fixed and fixed indexed annuities
- Guaranteed lifetime income options
- Best for: Pre-retirees and retirees wanting income certainty
- Minimum investment: $25,000

## Frequently Used Terms

- **Underwriting**: The process carriers use to evaluate risk and determine premiums
- **Premium**: Monthly or annual payment to keep a policy in force
- **Beneficiary**: Person(s) who receive the death benefit
- **Death Benefit**: The amount paid to beneficiaries upon the insured's passing
- **Cash Value**: The savings component of whole life/IUL policies
- **Rider**: Optional add-on coverage (waiver of premium, accidental death, child term)
- **Grace Period**: Typically 30-31 days after missed payment before policy lapses
- **Lapse**: When a policy terminates due to non-payment
- **Reinstatement**: Reactivating a lapsed policy (usually within 3-5 years)
- **Needs Analysis**: Assessment of how much and what type of coverage a client needs
- **In Force**: An active policy with current premiums paid
