## Business Processes

### PI Case Intake
1. Lead comes in (phone, web form, referral)
2. Legal assistant conducts intake call within 1 hour
3. Confirm statute of limitations (2 years in TX for PI)
4. Schedule free consultation (same day if possible)
5. Attorney reviews case viability
6. If accepted: sign retainer, send rep letter to insurance
7. Order police report and medical records
8. Begin treatment tracking

### PI Case Lifecycle
1. Treatment phase (client completes medical care)
2. Demand package prepared (records, bills, demand letter)
3. Demand sent to insurance company
4. Negotiation phase (30-60 days)
5. If no fair offer: file lawsuit
6. Discovery and depositions
7. Mediation
8. Trial (if mediation fails)
9. Settlement disbursement

### Family Law Intake
1. Free consultation (30 min phone or in-person)
2. Conflict check
3. Retainer agreement signed, payment collected
4. Initial petition drafted and filed
5. Opposing party served
6. Temporary orders hearing if needed
7. Discovery and negotiation
8. Mediation or trial

## Frequently Used Terms

- SOL: Statute of Limitations (deadline to file lawsuit)
- Rep Letter: Letter of Representation sent to insurance company
- Demand Package: Compilation of medical records, bills, and demand letter
- MedPay: Medical payments coverage on auto policy
- PIP: Personal Injury Protection
- UM/UIM: Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist coverage
- Contingency: Attorney fee based on percentage of recovery
- Retainer: Upfront deposit against hourly billing
- Discovery: Formal exchange of information between parties
- Deposition: Sworn testimony outside of court
- Mediation: Settlement negotiation with neutral third party
- TRO: Temporary Restraining Order
