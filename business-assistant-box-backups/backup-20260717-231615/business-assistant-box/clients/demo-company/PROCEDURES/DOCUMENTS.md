# DOCUMENTS.md

## Purpose

Define document creation standards for LifeLegacy Insurance.

---

## Document Types

### Proposal Letter
- Used when: presenting coverage recommendations to prospects
- Template: Company letterhead
- Includes: client name, recommended coverage, premium estimates, carrier comparison (2-3 options), next steps
- Disclaimer required: "Rates are estimates pending underwriting approval"
- Approval: Marcus reviews all proposals $100k+ before sending

### Needs Analysis Summary
- Used when: after completing a needs analysis consultation
- Includes: income, debts, dependents, coverage gap calculation, recommended coverage amount and type
- Saved to: client folder in Google Drive
- Naming: YYYY-MM-DD_LastName_NeedsAnalysis

### Policy Delivery Letter
- Used when: delivering an issued policy to client
- Includes: congratulations, coverage summary, premium schedule, beneficiary confirmation, contact info for questions
- Tone: warm and celebratory

### Annual Review Summary
- Used when: completing an annual policy review
- Includes: current coverage in force, changes in life circumstances, gaps identified, recommendations, next review date
- Naming: YYYY-MM-DD_LastName_AnnualReview

### Group Benefits Proposal
- Used when: presenting group life options to a business
- Includes: company info, employee census summary, coverage tiers, rate comparison, enrollment timeline, implementation plan
- Approval: Marcus must sign off before delivery

### Client Thank You Letter
- Used when: after policy issuance, after referral, after annual review
- Tone: genuine appreciation, brief
- Include referral program mention

---

## Formatting Rules

- All documents use company letterhead
- Font: clean and professional (no Comic Sans, no decorative fonts)
- File naming: YYYY-MM-DD_ClientLastName_DocumentType
- Save location: Google Drive > Clients > [Client Name] > Documents
- Also save copy in DOCUMENTS/company-documents if template-worthy

---

## Compliance

- All illustrations must include carrier-required disclosures
- No guaranteed language for market-linked products (IUL, variable)
- Include Georgia Department of Insurance license number on proposals
- Never include SSN or full health information in documents
- Encrypted delivery required for sensitive documents
